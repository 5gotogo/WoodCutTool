The problem: plywood waste starts before the first cut

Most wood waste is created during planning. A parts list may look correct on paper, but the pieces still have to fit on real plywood sheets with blade kerf, sheet size, grain direction, rotation limits, and usable offcuts. When the layout is guessed by hand, it is easy to buy too much material, miss a repeated panel, or create scraps that cannot be reused.

The solution: plan the sheet layout before cutting

CutList turns your woodworking parts list into a visual plywood cutting plan. Enter part dimensions, quantities, material details, and kerf, then preview how the pieces fit on each sheet. The result is a simpler way to check the layout, save the project, and carry the cut plan to the shop on your iPhone.

Why woodworkers use CutList

  • Cut optimization: organize part sizes and quantities into a clearer cutting layout before plywood reaches the saw.
  • Material saving: compare layouts, spot avoidable waste, and make better buying decisions before a project becomes expensive.
  • Plywood layout planning: plan cabinet sides, shelves, drawer boxes, backs, shop fixtures, closet panels, workbench tops, and other sheet-good projects.

Built for real workshop decisions

CutList is useful when a project has repeated parts, expensive sheet goods, or dimensions that keep changing. Cabinet makers, DIY builders, home renovators, garage shops, and small woodworking teams can use it to keep cut lists, plywood layouts, material settings, and PDF exports in one place.

Unlike a one-time sketch, a saved CutList project can be reopened when measurements change. Unlike a spreadsheet, the layout is visual, so you can review where each piece goes before cutting.

FAQ

What is a cut list optimizer?

A cut list optimizer is a tool that takes your part dimensions, quantities, sheet sizes, kerf, and material rules, then helps create a cutting layout. The goal is to make plywood layout planning easier, reduce mistakes, and use material more efficiently.

How do I reduce wood waste?

Reduce wood waste by measuring parts carefully, grouping similar pieces, accounting for saw kerf, checking grain direction, planning sheet layouts before cutting, and using a cut list optimizer to compare material usage before you buy or cut plywood.

Is CutList a plywood layout planning app?

Yes. CutList is built for plywood layout planning and sheet-good cut optimization, including saved projects, kerf settings, visual layouts, and PDF export.

Can CutList help with material saving?

Yes. CutList helps you review how parts fit on each sheet before cutting, so you can spot avoidable scraps, adjust dimensions, and make more confident material decisions.

Does CutList work offline?

Yes. CutList is designed for offline iPhone project planning, so your cut lists and plywood layouts can stay available in the workshop.

Which tools should I use with CutList?

Use the plywood cut calculator for fast online estimates, the cut list calculator for board projects, and the wood waste calculator to estimate material loss.
