CutList App - Offline Plywood Cut List Optimizer for iPhone

CutList is a cut list app for woodworkers who want an iPhone cut list optimizer that works without account setup, cloud upload, or a desktop spreadsheet. Build local projects, enter plywood parts, set saw kerf and rotation rules, preview sheet layouts, and export a PDF cut plan when the job is ready for the shop.

What CutList helps you do

CutList turns a parts list into a practical plywood sheet plan. Add cabinet sides, cabinet shelves, drawer boxes, backs, stretchers, shop fixtures, and DIY panels, then define the stock sheet size and saw kerf. The app helps you see how material is consumed before you cut, so a cut list becomes a visual layout instead of a spreadsheet guess.

Who uses CutList

CutList is built for cabinet makers, small woodworking shops, garage builders, home renovators, van conversion builders, and DIY woodworking users who need a plywood cut list app that stays simple. It is especially useful when repeated parts make manual layout slow: shelf pins, drawer boxes, closet organizers, bookcases, workbench tops, and utility cabinets.

Key features

  • iPhone cut list optimizer for plywood and sheet goods.
  • Local projects for saved cut lists and repeat edits.
  • Saw kerf, rotation, material, and sheet setup controls.

Offline and privacy-first workflow

CutList is designed around local projects. The core planning workflow does not require an account and does not depend on a cloud upload. That matters for shops that do not want client dimensions, cabinet notes, or material lists scattered across shared services. You can plan in the app, keep the project on the device, and only export or share when you choose.

CutList vs spreadsheet

A spreadsheet can calculate quantities, but it is not a natural sheet layout tool. It will not show how a 30 by 18 inch shelf competes with a 34.5 by 23.25 inch side panel on a 4 by 8 sheet, and it is easy to forget kerf or rotation rules. CutList gives the spatial part of the job a visual interface while still keeping the workflow lightweight.

Online calculator vs iPhone app

Use the free plywood cutting calculator for quick planning in the browser. Use CutList when the project should be saved, edited later, carried to the saw, exported as a PDF, or kept offline on an iPhone. The online calculator is a fast estimate; the iPhone app is the project workspace.

FAQ

What is CutList?

CutList is an offline iPhone cut list optimizer for plywood and sheet goods. It helps woodworkers enter parts, define stock, account for kerf, preview layouts, and keep projects locally.

Who should use a plywood cut list app?

Cabinet makers, DIY woodworkers, built-in installers, and small shops use a plywood cut list app when a project has repeated panels, shelves, drawer boxes, or sheet layout decisions.

Does CutList require an account or cloud upload?

No account is required for the core workflow, and local projects do not need a cloud upload.

Can CutList export PDF cut plans?

Yes. CutList supports PDF export so project layouts can be reviewed, shared, or printed for the shop.

When should I use the online calculator instead?

Use the online calculator for fast sheet count and waste checks. Use the app when you need saved local projects, repeat edits, PDF export, and offline access.
