CutList App - Offline Plywood Cut List Optimizer for iPhone

CutList is an iPhone cut list app for woodworkers who need more than a quick online estimate. Use it to save cut lists, keep local project records, preview plywood sheet layouts, set saw kerf and material notes, and export cut list PDF plans for the workshop. It is built for cabinet makers, DIY builders, and small shops that want an offline woodworking calculator with no account required and no cloud upload for the core project workflow. Open it when a layout needs to travel with the job.

What CutList helps you do

CutList turns a parts list into a practical plywood sheet plan that stays with the project. Add cabinet sides, shelves, drawer box parts, backs, stretchers, shop fixtures, or DIY panels, then define the stock sheet size and material details. The goal is not just to calculate once; it is to keep a reliable cutting plan on your iPhone as the job changes.

Who uses CutList

CutList is built for cabinet makers, small woodworking shops, garage builders, home renovators, van conversion builders, and DIY woodworking users who need a plywood cut list app that stays simple. It is especially useful when repeated parts make manual layout slow: cabinet boxes, closet organizers, bookcases, shop cabinets, workbench tops, and utility shelving.

Key features

Visual sheet layout

CutList helps you move from rows of dimensions to a visual sheet layout. That makes it easier to catch an odd panel, an impossible grouping, or a layout that creates awkward offcuts before you start cutting.

Saved local projects

Each job can live as a local project instead of a loose note or one-time browser calculation. Save cut lists for different cabinets, shop fixtures, closet organizers, and plywood builds, then reopen them when measurements change.

Saw kerf and material planning

Real cutting plans need blade kerf, material thickness, sheet size, rotation choices, and material notes. CutList keeps those setup decisions close to the parts list so the cutting plan reflects the actual workshop process.

PDF export

Use PDF export when a cut list needs to be reviewed, printed, shared, or kept near the saw. A PDF cutting plan is easier to hand off than a spreadsheet and more durable than a rough sketch.

No account, no cloud upload

CutList is designed as a privacy-first woodworking app. The core workflow uses local project storage with no account required and no cloud upload needed to plan or revise your cut lists.

Why use an app instead of a spreadsheet?

A spreadsheet can hold dimensions, but it does not naturally behave like a sheet cutting app. Cabinet makers often need to revise quantities, reopen older jobs, carry the plan to the shop, and export a clean cut list PDF. CutList keeps the woodworking project calculator workflow on the phone instead of spreading it across formulas, screenshots, and paper notes.

Online calculator vs CutList iPhone app

Use the free plywood cutting calculator for quick planning in the browser when you only need a rough estimate. Use CutList when the project should be saved, edited later, carried to the saw, exported as a PDF, or kept offline on an iPhone. The online plywood cut calculator is a fast estimate; the iPhone app is the project workspace. You can also try the browser-based sheet cutting calculator before deciding whether a job needs the app.

Privacy-first woodworking workflow

Shop dimensions, client notes, and cabinet part lists can be sensitive. CutList keeps the core workflow local, with no account required and no cloud upload needed for normal project planning. You choose when to export or share a cutting plan.

Common projects

CutList works well for plywood and sheet-good projects such as cabinet sides, adjustable shelves, drawer boxes, back panels, shop cabinets, garage shelving, closet organizers, van storage, workbench tops, and small built-ins. It can also support MDF, melamine, and other rectangular sheet materials when the correct stock dimensions are entered.

FAQ

What is CutList?

CutList is an offline iPhone cut list optimizer for plywood and sheet goods. It helps woodworkers enter parts, define stock, account for kerf, preview layouts, and keep projects locally.

Is CutList a plywood cut list app?

Yes. CutList is designed for plywood and sheet-good cut lists, including sheet layouts, kerf settings, saved local projects, and PDF export.

Does CutList work offline?

Can I export a cut list as PDF?

Can I save multiple projects?
