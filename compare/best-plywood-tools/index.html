Tool comparison

Best Plywood Layout Tools for Cut Planning

The best plywood tool depends on project complexity. A quick sketch can work for one shelf. A visual optimizer becomes more useful when parts repeat, kerf matters, grain direction matters, or plywood is expensive.

Paper Sketch

Good for simple projects and rough thinking, but easy to miscalculate kerf or miss a better layout.

Spreadsheet

Useful for quantities and costs, but weak for spatial layout because cells do not show how parts actually fit on a sheet.

CutList Optimizer

Best for visual plywood cutting optimization. It helps review part placement, waste, sequence, and material decisions before cutting.

Recommended Workflow

Read how to cut plywood efficiently, sketch your first constraints with the 4x8 plywood sheet template, then use CutList for the final layout.
