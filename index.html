Why a cut list is not enough

WoodCutTool is built around a practical problem every cabinet maker, shop owner, and DIY woodworking builder runs into: a parts list is not the same thing as a sheet layout. A plywood cutting calculator needs to turn cabinet shelves, drawer boxes, side panels, backs, and utility panels into a cut plan that respects the real sheet size, the saw kerf, and the order in which material can be handled safely.

Preview the sheet layout before cutting

The browser tool works as a fast sheet cutting calculator for early decisions. Enter the sheet size, add part dimensions and quantities, choose whether parts may rotate, and preview how the layout affects yield. Instead of guessing from square inches alone, the cut list optimizer helps you see whether a job fits on two sheets, needs a third sheet, or creates offcuts that are worth saving. That matters before you buy material, especially when plywood price, grain direction, and finished faces make mistakes expensive.

Online planning first, CutList app for serious jobs

For many projects, the best workflow is to use WoodCutTool as a woodworking calculator during planning, then move serious jobs into CutList for iPhone. The online calculator is useful for quick quotes, garage shelving, closet organizers, built-ins, and shop fixtures where you want to reduce waste before cutting. The offline iPhone app is better when the project needs saved local records, repeat edits, PDF export, and a cut list you can keep at the saw without a cloud upload or account.

Built for plywood optimization first

WoodCutTool starts with plywood optimization: plan the material, check the layout, understand the waste, then save the final cut list. Other maker calculators are available from the navigation, but CutList remains the core workflow for woodworking projects that need clean sheet layouts and reliable cutting plans. FAQ

Quick answers before you plan a cut list.

What is the fastest way to estimate plywood for a project?

Start with the plywood cutting calculator for a quick sheet count, then review the visual layout for kerf, rotation, grain direction, and usable offcuts before buying material.

When should I use CutList instead of a basic calculator?

Use CutList when the project has repeated parts, multiple materials, saved revisions, PDF export, local project history, or a cut plan you want to keep at the saw.

Can WoodCutTool help with cabinet and built-in projects?

Yes. Start with the kitchen cabinet cut list template or browse all plywood cut list templates for bookcases, closet and garage shelving, drawer boxes, workbenches, and more.

Does the online calculator require an account or upload?

No. The online planning flow is browser based and designed for quick no-login plywood layout checks before you move serious saved projects into the CutList app.
