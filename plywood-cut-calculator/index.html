How to use this plywood cutting calculator

Start with the actual plywood sheet size you plan to buy, not a nominal size from memory. A common full sheet is 96 by 48 inches, but imported panels, prefinished sheets, and home-center stock can vary. Enter the saw kerf for the blade you will use, then add every part with a clear label, finished length, finished width, and quantity. If a part has visible grain direction or a finished face, turn rotation off so the sheet layout does not trade quality for a small waste reduction.

After running the calculator, review the sheet count before you review the waste number. A layout that saves two percent but forces one more sheet is not a better buying decision. Then look at the first-sheet preview and the cut sequence. Thin strips, fragile long rips, and awkward offcuts are signs that the plan may need a different stock size or a second material group.

Example: cabinet shelf layout

Imagine a small base cabinet project with two 34.5 by 23.25 inch side panels, three 30 by 18 inch cabinet shelves, and one 30 by 4 inch stretcher. On a 96 by 48 inch sheet with a 1/8 inch kerf, the calculator can estimate whether the parts fit on one sheet and how much usable offcut remains. The common mistake is to add only the visible shelves and forget stretchers, nailers, or drawer box parts. That makes the waste estimate look better than the real project.

What is saw kerf?

Saw kerf is the width of plywood removed by the blade during each cut. If your blade removes 0.125 inches, every cut line consumes that width. Ignoring kerf can make a digital sheet layout look possible while the final part is short in the shop. For clean cabinet work, enter the blade you will actually use and include any trim cut needed to square a rough factory edge.

How to reduce plywood waste

Reducing waste is not only about packing rectangles tightly. Group parts by material, lock rotation where grain matters, batch repeated cabinet shelves, and decide which offcuts are worth saving. A useful offcut should have enough size and a clear label; otherwise it becomes clutter. FAQ

What is a plywood cut calculator?

A plywood cut calculator converts sheet size, part dimensions, quantities, and saw kerf into an estimated sheet layout, cut sequence, sheet count, and waste percentage.

How do I calculate plywood waste?

Compare the total area of purchased sheets with the area used by parts and cut spacing. The calculator estimates the unused area after the layout is packed.

What is saw kerf?

Saw kerf is the blade width removed during cutting. It must be included so parts do not end up too small.

Can I rotate parts in the layout?

Yes. Use the rotation setting when grain direction and face orientation do not matter. Turn it off for visible cabinet parts.

Can I save plywood cut layouts?

The browser tool is meant for quick estimates. Use CutList for iPhone to save layouts as local projects.

Is this calculator private?
